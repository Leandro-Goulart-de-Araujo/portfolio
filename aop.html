Advanced Oxidation Processes

Project Summary: This section gathers projects involving the degradation of organic pollutants through processes such as ozonation, UV/H2O2, electron beam irradiation, and Fenton's reagent. It includes kinetic modeling, materials evaluation, and experimental design approaches.

Key Contributions:

  • Developed and modeled UV/H2O2 degradation of bisphenol A using experimental design and kinetic approaches
  • Evaluated photocatalysts (TiO2) under solar simulation for atrazine degradation
  • Applied ozonation in batch mode to treat textile wastewater and radioactive oil sludge
  • Studied the degradation of ion exchange resins and organic sludges using Fenton and e-beam techniques

Publications:

  • TiO2 P25 and Kronos vlp 7000 materials activated by simulated solar light for atrazine degradation – IJCRE (2023)
  • Physicochemical Modifications and Decolorization of Textile Wastewater by Ozonation – Ozone: Science & Engineering (2023)
  • Preliminary studies on electron beam irradiation as a treatment method of radioactive oil sludge – BJRS (2021)
  • Degradation of bisphenol A by the UV/H2O2 process: a kinetic study – ESPR (2020)
  • Physicochemical modifications of radioactive oil sludge by ozone treatment – JECE (2020)
  • Reaction of Ion Exchange Resins with Fenton's Reagent – Environments (2018)
  • Degradation of Bisphenol A by the UV and UV/H2O2 Processes – CJCE (2017)
