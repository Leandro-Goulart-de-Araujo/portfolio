ML in Adsorption

Project Summary: This work highlights how machine learning has been applied to model, predict, and optimize adsorption processes for various pollutants using a range of biosorbents and synthetic materials.

Key Contributions:

  • Lead author of an article combining experimental and ML modeling of methylene blue biosorption using bone meal
  • Applied experimental design, ANN, and feature analysis to explain biosorption behavior
  • Co-author in a study combining ML, experimental design, and clustering to understand uranium removal using hydroxyapatite and bone meal
  • Currently developing a ML-driven framework to predict adsorption of dyes (Reactive Black 5) onto biochars using Random Forest and other algorithms

Publications:

  • Biosorption of methylene blue by bone meal: experimental and modeling with machine learning and full factorial design – Front. Environ. Chem. (2024)
  • Uranium biosorption by hydroxyapatite and bone meal: evaluation of process variables through experimental design – ESPR (2021)
  • Removal of Reactive Black 5 Dye by Biochars: Experimental and Data-Driven Modeling Approaches (Under preparation)
